My Evolution

How BCOM 314 changed the way I see myself — and how others see me.

This semester, I collected data through Radian's Self-Perception Inventory (SPI) and Professional Reputation Survey (PRS) to better understand the gap between how I see myself and how others experience me. What I found surprised me — and gave me a clearer picture of the professional I'm becoming.

01

Communication Style

My Self-Perception Inventory identified my Attitudinal Communication Style as Analytical — process-oriented, data-driven, and structured. My composite Proactivity score was −3.0 and Reactivity score was −2.0, placing me in the lower-left quadrant. I see myself as methodical and deliberate, someone who thinks carefully before acting. But when my Professional Reputation Survey came back, others told a different story entirely. My average Behavioral Communication Style from 4 respondents was Expressive — with a Composite Proactivity of 5.0 and Reactivity of 1.0. To the people around me, I show up as outgoing, assertive, and energetic. This gap was one of the most eye-opening discoveries of the semester. It tells me that while I may feel measured and reserved internally, I project confidence and energy externally — a genuine asset in analytics and BI roles where communication and presence matter as much as technical skill.
